MERRIWEATHER

History

Eben Sorkin created the Merriweather font, which was first released on July 22, 2011. Sorkin's goal was to create a font that was legible and pleasant to read at various sizes, while also adapting traditional book typefaces for modern screens. Eben is a type designer and type publisher living in Boston, Massachusetts, USA. His company is Sorkin Type Co. It was inspired by specific book typefaces like Garamond. The creator, Eben Sorkin, wanted to adapt old fashion book style to modern screens, but also bring in something genuinely new to the style. This would mean the font should be legible and pleasant to read at a variation of sizes.Highly readable on screenUpon careful inspection and comparison of Merriweather to other serif typefaces, it features a larger x height than most fonts. The stems of each letter have no entasis, meaning they are vertical and straight, rather than bending in like a curve. This feature helps make it look cleaner at smaller font sizes. Most typefaces designed for screens use vertical stress but by using a diagonal stress, it maintains the old fashion book type feel.
